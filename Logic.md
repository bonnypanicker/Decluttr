### Why Declutter-First Wins (Not Pure Archive-First)
- **Users search and download for declutter**, not “archive”. Real data (2026):
  - People Google “declutter android apps”, “free up space android”, “remove unused apps”, “phone slow storage full”.
  - Almost nobody searches “app archive” or “app vault” as their first need.
  - Google’s own built-in archiving is marketed exactly as “declutter your phone / save space without deleting”.
- Your second screenshot (“Rarely Used (19)” with checkboxes + sizes + “Show All Apps”) is **pure gold** — instant “I need this now” feeling.
- Your first screenshot (grid + huge green “Declutter 19 Apps” button + “~135MB saved” badge) already screams value.
- The **Archive (“My Archive”)** is the magic that makes people stay and love the app long-term. Most cleaner apps just delete forever. You save them safely. That’s your unfair advantage.

If you made “My Archive” the very first screen, people would open the app, see a half-empty library, and think “not for me yet”. Declutter-first gives instant win + teaches the archive benefit.
